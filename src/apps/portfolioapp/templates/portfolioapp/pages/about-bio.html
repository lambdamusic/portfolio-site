About
Hello and welcome to my personal site. I am Michele Pasin, a software engineer and web developer with a background in academic research. My areas of specialism include data analytics, data architecture, interactive information exploration, ontology engineering and knowledge graphs.

I currently work at Digital Science, an organization whose mission is to help researchers and the broader scientific world do things faster - and better - thanks to innovative software products. My focus and expertise is Dimensions, an amazing research intelligence platform used by many organizations around the world in order to discover, evaluate, assess and gather competitive intelligence about scientific research.

Prior to that, I worked at Springer Nature as an information & data architect and developed projects such as SN Scigraph, Nature.com subject pages and the underlying Nature Ontologies portal.

I hold a PhD in semantic web technologies from the Knowledge Media Institute (The Open University, UK) and advanced degrees in logic and philosophy of language from the University of Venice (Italy). After my doctorate, I spent various years as a research associate at King's College Department of Digital Humanities (London), where I worked on digital humanities projects such as the People of Medieval Scotland and the Art of Making in Antiquity.
