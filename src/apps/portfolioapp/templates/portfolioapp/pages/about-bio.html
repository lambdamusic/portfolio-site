About
Hello and welcome to my personal site. I am a philosopher turned computer scientist, with a long-standing passion for making music.

My areas of specialism are:

  • Information & data architecture
  • Visual analytics
  • Artificial intelligence, in particular symbolic AI, ontology-based systems and knowledge graphs
  • Python and web development

    • In general, I am interested in the intersection of technology and research. How can we design digital tools that facilitate research and knowledge work?

    I currently work at Digital Science, an organization whose mission is to help researchers and the broader scientific world do things faster - and better - thanks to innovative software products. My focus and expertise is Dimensions.ai, a state-of-the-art scientific research assessment and discovery platform.

    Prior to that, I worked at Springer Nature as an information & data architect and developed projects such as SN Scigraph, Nature.com subject pages and the underlying Nature Ontologies portal.

    I hold a PhD in semantic web technologies from the Knowledge Media Institute (The Open University, UK) and advanced degrees in logic and philosophy of language from the University of Venice (Italy). After my doctorate, I spent various years as a research associate at King's College Department of Digital Humanities (London), where I worked on digital humanities projects such as the People of Medieval Scotland and the Art of Making in Antiquity.
