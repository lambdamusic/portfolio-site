About
I'm a knowledge engineer at heart and have been dealing with the problem of eliciting, representing, organising and exploring knowledge via digital means for 20+ years and manyfold technologies.

I currently work at Digital Science, an organization whose mission is to help researchers and the broader scientific world do things faster - and better - thanks to innovative software products. My focus and expertise is Dimensions. Dimensions has been descibed as the worldâ€™s largest linked research information dataset. It is a business intelligence platform used by organizations in order to discover, evaluate, assess and gather competitive intelligence about research-related topics across the disciplines.

Prior to that, I worked at Springer Nature as an information & data architect and developed projects such as SN Scigraph, Nature.com subject pages and the underlying Nature Ontologies portal.

I hold a PhD in semantic web technologies from the Knowledge Media Institute (The Open University, UK) and advanced degrees in logic and philosophy of language from the University of Venice (Italy). After my doctorate, I spent various years as a research associate at King's College Department of Digital Humanities (London), where I worked on influential digital humanities projects such as the People of Medieval Scotland and the Art of Making in Antiquity.
