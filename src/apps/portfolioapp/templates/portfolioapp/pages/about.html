About :: The following bio can be used for events and workshops

Michele Pasin is a data scientist with a focus on data architecture, enterprise metadata management and semantic technologies.

Michele works for Digital Science, a technology company focusing on strategic investments into startup companies that support the research lifecycle. At Digital Science, Michele is the product lead for the Dimensions Analytics API, a research analytics tool that allows to draw statistics and insights from the millions of publications, grants, clinical trials and patents indexed by Dimensions.ai.

Prior to that, Michele worked at Springer Nature where he led the development of Springer Nature’s Scigraph project, a large and highly integrated open linked data platform. Also, he was instrumental in projects like Nature’s subject pages (a dynamic section of the website that allow users to navigate content by topic) and the ontologies portal (a public repository of knowledge models used at Nature).

Michele holds a PhD in semantic web technologies from the Knowledge Media Institute (The Open University, UK) and advanced degrees in logic and philosophy of language from the University of Venice (Italy). Previously, he was a research associate at King's College Department of Digital Humanities (London), where he contributed to influential digital humanities projects such as the People of Medieval Scotland and the Art of Making in Antiquity.
